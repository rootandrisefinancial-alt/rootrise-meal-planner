📊 Marathon Pace Check
Goal of 4:45 = 6:44/km. Your M-pace training is exactly right. As VDOT improves through training, this pace will feel easier by October.
🏔️ Trail Pace Note
For all trail long runs, run by effort only — ignore pace. Add ~60–90 sec/km for technical terrain and climbs. Trail sessions are time-on-feet, not pace benchmarks.
💪 Strength for Runners — Session Guide
🦵 Lower Body
Bulgarian split squats 3×8, Romanian deadlifts 3×8, step-ups 3×10, hip thrusts 3×12, single-leg calf raises 3×15. Focus: unilateral strength, glute drive, eccentric control for downhills. Never within 24hrs of a quality run or long run.
💪 Upper Body
Push-ups or bench press 3×10, bent-over rows 3×10, shoulder press 3×10, lat pull-down 3×12, plank 3×30s, dead bug 3×10. Focus: running posture, arm drive, core. Most flexible — can pair with easy run days.
🏋️ Full Body
Deadlift 3×8, goblet squat 3×10, push-up 3×12, dumbbell row 3×10, reverse lunge 3×10, glute bridge 3×15. Focus: total body durability and running economy.
📉 Taper Protocol
Week 14: upper body only, 2 sets. Week 15 (race week): zero strength. Full neuromuscular freshness is the goal. After races: upper body in 2–3 days; lower body waits until legs feel 100%.
🏔️ Trail Running Guidelines
Run by effort, not pace
Ignore your watch pace on trails. Easy trail effort = easy road effort, even if you're 2+ min/km slower.
Power-hike steep climbs
No poles = quads do all the work. Power-hiking (hands on knees) is faster and more economical than shuffling uphill. Practice every trail long run.
Control descents
Finlayson Arm has 1,500m of descent on tired legs. Practice controlled, quick-foot descents every trail run — biggest race-day risk without poles.
Thetis / Witty's Lagoon
Moderate vert. Use for easy trail days and long runs where you want climb stimulus without hammering your body pre-quality.
Goldstream / Mt. Finlayson
Big vert simulation. Target at least 2 long runs here (Weeks 5 and 9). Week 9 = Finlayson sim — aim for 800–1,000m gain.
Trail vs. road split
Trail long runs: Weeks 1, 5, 8, 9. Road long runs (M-pace): Weeks 4, 10, 13, 14. Both are essential.
Run Workout Types
Easy (E)
~80% of all weekly km. Conversational pace. Should feel almost too easy.
Marathon Pace (M)
Your goal race pace. Purposeful but sustainable for hours. Core of long run quality work.
Threshold (T)
Cruise intervals or tempo. Raises lactate threshold — the key to holding marathon pace.
Interval (I)
Hard 1–5min reps at VO2max. Boosts aerobic ceiling. Used mid-plan in Phase III.
Long Run (L)
Always at E or M pace. Time on feet. The cornerstone of marathon training.
Daniels' Key Rules
80% Easy
At least 80% of weekly km at Easy pace. Don't cheat this — even on weeks with 3 strength sessions.
2 Quality Days Max
Never more than 2 hard run sessions per week. Always separate with easy days.
Race Recovery
1 easy day per 3km of race distance before returning to quality running or lower body strength.
Long Run Cap
Long run ≤ 25% of weekly distance or 150 minutes, whichever comes first.
Listen to Your Body
Flat or sore? Drop strength before dropping the run. Quality over quantity, always.
